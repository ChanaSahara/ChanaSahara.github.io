Travel To The UP!

What is the UP?

Michigan is a state in the US, commonly known as "the mitten state", because it is shaped, surprisingly, like a mitten.
So, what's the UP? UP stands for Upper Peninsula.
That's right! Michigan is unique because it has two peninsulas.
In this trip guide we will be focusing on the UP (upper peninsula). Read on for more information!

Why should I travel to the UP?

The UP is a truly lovely place. Michigan is known for it's great lakes, being the Great Lake State. The UP is surrounded by three of the great lakes;
Lake Superior, Lake Huron, and of course Lake Michigan. There are many campgrounds to stay in if you want more of a rustic experience,
or if you perfer sleeping indoors there are also hotels.

One thing that stands out about the UP is the population. Although certain places attract many tourists, the population is small
compared to the lower peninsula. This makes it a wonderful place to travel...you can make your vacation calm and relaxing,
or if you choose, you can also mingle with crowds at sight-seeing spots.

Following are some pictures, so you know just what you're getting when you book your flight.

A sand dune is a hill or mountain of sand, formed by the waves of the great lakes washing up sand, and
the wind blowing in into great mountain like structures. Although the UP isn't known for it's dunes, (as the lower peninsula is, being home
to the famous Sleeping Bear Dunes), there are still many impressive dunes. One is the Log Slide Overlook, which
is exactl what ist sounds like. Sand dunes can be great "play grounds" for kids, as well as a good, long hike.

Another special thing about the UP is the sunsets. Some spots are better then others, but no matter where you're staying
you can most likely find a spot close by with a beautiful view. This is partly due to much less light polution, as well as
not as many tall buildings blocking the view.

And, of course, the beaches. The UP is full of beaches, and they very a lot.
There are many large, standard beaches that attract crowds, but there are also small, more private ones like this.
You can see there's not much sand, but there is a nice slope so the water doesn't get too deep all at once like it often will.
There's also planty of shade, which large beaches don't often offer. The nice thing about the UP is that you can choose what
environment you want...large and bursting with people to meet, or small and private to enjoy yourselves.
